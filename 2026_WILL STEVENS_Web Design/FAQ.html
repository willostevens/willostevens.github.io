FAQ:

  • Where can I contact you?

  • What application do you use to draw?
    • I use the app Procreate, with an ipad and apple pen.
  • Are you currently open for work or internships?
    • Unfortunately I am not, as I'm still trudging my way through school!
  • Any advice for new artists?
    • Don't get caught in the technicalities! The biggest danger of art is abandoning passion for skill. If you don't love the process and the final product equally then you need to figure out how before it's too late. You'll always have bad days, but if you find yourself coming back regardless then you know your passion still sparks!
  • Where do you get your inspiration?
    • God, too many places at once, haha! A lot of my inspiration for art and characters comes from music and what I feel as a result, that's why you'll find a good portion of my characters have specific songs linked to their design and descriptions. For general inspiration, pinterest is of course always fun to go through (but you do have to be more wary nowadays, AI is everywhere). Don't underestimate the power of just going outside! Colours and patterns are everywhere! They're engrained in our planet! You'll find everything you need if you look around.
  • Thoughts on AI?
    • I refuse to associate with any generated content, and I refuse to use AI for any of my artwork or writing, even as a tool or assistant. Additionally, I refuse to work for or encourage any studios or peoples who do use this. The entertainment industry runs on the backs of the passionate, and the usage of tools enforces a stupidity designed to opress us. I will reject AI or I will die trying.
  • Are you working on anything?
    • I'm currently dipping my toes in animation! It's what I want to pursue in the future but I've never had much experience with it. I'm doing stuff without tutorials or assistance right now, so I can look it over when I'm done and identify what needs the most improvement. I'm currently working on this animation displayed on the left, of the characters Coco and Agott from Witch Hat Atelier to a short snippet from the song 'Blue Eyes Like the Devils Water' by McCafferty. As you can see it's quite messy haha, but I'm hoping to colour it in and use it as my reference going forward for my current level of capability. I already know a bit from observation, but ask me to name any actual techniques and I'll come up blank! It's a bit embarassing to admit that it's taken this long to actually delve into the depths of animation beyond short, random things here and there, when it's been part of my dream career for years. It's very easy, I think, to get caught up in perfecting the littler things like linework or anatomy or perspective, and you get so caught up into juggling all these minute details that you forget the bigger picture. I'm always going to find something in my art to scrutinise, and it's a good skill to have, but I have to acknowledge the strengths I have (even if they don't feel like strengths yet) in order to focus on what I lack. I'm going forward with hope! Wish me luck :)
  • What are you using to animate?
    • While usually I just use procreate's built in animation function, I've found myself going back to my roots: Flipaclip! The video above is animated fully in flipaclip. The ads are as frustrating as ever, but the ability to add music to the video and have easy layers is wonderful!
